    Problem Solving

    Got blocked on a problem

    I got blocked this sprint on the "SuperFizzBuzz" problem.
    The problem was, that I did not know where to start with what. I know the logic, but I did not know what syntax or methods to use to code and create the outcome that was needed.
    Some problem solving techniques I did were, Take a break. I went and did something completely different to try and refresh. I then googled to see what I could find, This ended up with me looking at to much and then second guessing myself again. Failing this, I talked to my squishy I have on my desk, And again I could explain the logic but couldnt think of where to start. Since I still was getting nowhere, I reached out to my peers, and asked for help. They were very helpful in giving me hints in ways of how I should be thinking and how to go about solving the problem. With some progress, I took another break. Coming back with a clear head, I was able to solve the problem.
    I felt very fustrated throughout the process, As I could not get my logic out into syntax. This led me to thinking, That I actually do not know what I am doing, And had me second guessing myself if i actually am learning anything, And applying that knowledge. As seeing everybody else moving along with the curriculum, and yet I was still stuck, Made me think maybe I am not up to speed with all this.
    I learnt that, I should take more breaks often, I also learnt that reaching out for help is ok, And that my peers are more then willing to help me achieve an outcome.

    Solved a problem in elegant way

    I guess an elegant way of solving a problem, is talking it out loud to my squishy. This problem I had was if I was wondering if I should ask for help or not - As I have a hardtime interacting with people. For a problem solving technique I talked to my squishy, As if I was asking for help. This made it easier for me to reach out and ask for help, As I had already known what I was going to say, And felt good about it as it was easy talking to my squishy about the problem, I just had to relay that back in chat. I learnt that its OK to ask for help, everyone gets stuck, And our peers are more then willing to lean a suggestion or a hint to let you know how to go about the certain problem.

    Problem Solving Techniques

    Pseudocode

    I havent used Pseudocode much, Definately something I need to practice more, As I find it can help get things out of your head, Also give you steps to follow in roughly how to go about things. I am not to confident using Pseudocode but am looking forward to giving it more thought.

    Trying something

    Im confident in trying something, Most the time I try somthing and it doesnt work, But atleast I know it doesnt, Or it will give me a error , that I can then work out from there. Sometimes with trying something to often with it returning errors all the time, Gets me abit disgruntled, and this is where I turn to another method.

    Rubber ducky method

    I am confident with the rubber ducky method, I use this almost daily, (I have a squishy). This helps me to break things down into a really simple form. Again if this still does not work, I go to google.

    Reading error messages

    Semi confident in readign error messages, As it usually tells you what is wrong/where. Sometimes I get confused with what it is returning or asking with certain syntax. I hope to improve on this.

    Console.logging

    Console.logging I am not to confident in, Mainly as I barely use it, over this sprint I found myself wanting to use it more to get an indication of where I was at. I found that console logging often, Can show you what your code is doing and give you an indication of what to do next.

    Googling

    Google is King! - Although sometimes I find myself looking at to many solutions to the problem I am trying to solve, and this leaves me more confused then when I started searching.

    Asking your peers for help

    I am not confident asking my peers for help, I usually do as a last resort, After I have tried other problem solving techniques. This is because, I know my peers are most likely busy, and I dont like to contact people unless I absoulately need to. When I do contact, this is usually over direct message, not in the public channels. As I feel it is better just to have one input to bounce off.

    Asking coaches for help

    I have not done this yet, I may in future, This would be my last resort I think.
